Lean Business Plan — Deadstock Revival Co.
1. Executive Summary / Identity

Identity:
Deadstock Revival Co. sources unused, discontinued, or unsold clothing from small and unpopular brands and resells them through a modern online platform.

2. Company Description / Problem + Solution

Problem:
Small and unpopular clothing brands often have leftover inventory they cannot sell. Consumers want unique, sustainable clothing but don’t know where to find it.

Solution:
Deadstock Revival Co. purchases or partners to acquire deadstock clothing, photographs and rebrands it, and sells it on a stylish, easy-to-use website focused on sustainability and uniqueness.

3. Market Analysis / Target Market

Target Market:
• Teens and young adults (13–30) who like thrift culture, sustainability, and unique fashion
• Online shoppers looking for vintage-style or limited-quantity clothing
• Eco-conscious buyers who prefer reuse over fast fashion

4. Competitive Analysis / The Competition

The Competition:
• Thrift and resale sites like Depop, Poshmark, and Grailed
• Vintage clothing stores
• Small boutique resellers on Instagram or TikTok

Competitive Advantage:
• Focus only on deadstock (brand-new, but discontinued)
• Unique supply from small/unpopular brands — not mass-market
• Higher quality control since everything is new, not worn
• Lower prices than most vintage retailers

5. Market Plan / Marketing Activities

Marketing Activities:
• Promote items through TikTok and Instagram Reels
• Weekly style drops (build hype like “deadstock Fridays”)
• Collaborations with small creators who like fashion
• Email newsletter for new drop announcements
• Website with lookbooks and curated outfits

6. Management Team / Team & Key Roles

Team and Key Roles:
• Founder: Runs sourcing, website updates, photography, packaging, and shipping
• Future roles as business grows:
– Social media manager
– Inventory/supplier coordinator
– Photographer or stylist

7. Financial Plan / Expenses + Revenue Streams

Expenses:
• Buying deadstock clothing from brands
• Website hosting + domain
• Packaging materials
• Photography equipment / lighting
• Shipping costs
• Marketing ads

Revenue Streams:
• Direct sales of deadstock clothing through the website
• Occasional limited-edition collections
• Future: brand partnerships or collaborations

Milestones:
• Launch website and upload first 50 items
• Build monthly following of 500+ customers
• Add new brands and increase inventory
• Expand to international shipping
